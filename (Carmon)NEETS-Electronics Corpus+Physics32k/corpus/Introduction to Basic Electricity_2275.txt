When the current changes direction or stops, or 
the magnetic field changes, an electromotive force 
(emf) is induced back into the conductor through the 
collapsing of the magnetic field. The opposition to 
the changes in current flow is identified as a counter 
electromotive force (counter emf). lenz’s	law sum-
marizes this effect—an induced emf in any circuit is 
always in a direction to oppose the effect that pro-
duced it. The amount of counter emf is in proportion 
to the rate of change: The faster the rate of change, the 
greater the counter emf.
