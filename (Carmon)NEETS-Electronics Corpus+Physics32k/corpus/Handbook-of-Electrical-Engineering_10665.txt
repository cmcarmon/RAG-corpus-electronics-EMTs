Launched  towards  the  end  of  1997,  Cassini  will  make  ﬂybys  of  Venus  and  Jupiter  en  route  to  a
rendezvous with Saturn in July 2004. Cassini will release the Huygens Probe during its ﬁrst orbit, then
make  approximately  40  revolutions  over  a  span  of  four  years,  while  the  spacecraft’s  12  instruments
conduct a detailed exploration of the whole Saturnian system, including Titan and the planet’s other icy
moons. (Courtesy of National Aeronautics and Space Administration.)
