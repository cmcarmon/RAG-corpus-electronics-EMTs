April 12, 1960 A Cuban Vickers Viscount is hijacked by three crew members and a passenger, demanding political asylum in the United States.
July 5, 1960 A Cuban Bristol Britannia 138 is seized by two co-pilots and diverted to Miami.
July 28, 1960 The captain of Cubana DC-3 and two passengers forced the co-pilot out of the cockpit. The captain diverted the plane to Miami and demanded political asylum.
October 29, 1960 Cubana Flight 905 is hijacked by the co-pilot who took an air marshal hostage. A shooting killed the marshal. The co-pilot and nine passengers involved in the hijack request asylum in the United States.
December 8, 1960 A Cuban plane crash landed after five Cubans attempted to hijack the plane to the United States. A gun battle killed one person before the flight crashed.
January 1, 1961 a Cuban Bristol Britannia is hijacked to the US.
May 1, 1961 Antulio Ramirez Ortiz hijacks a National Airlines Flight 337 from Miami International Airport to Cuba.
July 3, 1961 14 hijackers diverted a DC-3 to the United States.
July 24, 1961 Eastern Air Lines Flight 202, a Lockheed L-188 Electra, is hijacked to Cuba.
July 31, 1961 Pacific Air Lines Flight 327 is hijacked by a Bruce Britt Sr., demanding to be taken to Cuba. The pilot and a ticket agent were both shot by the hijacker, who was overpowered by the copilot and passengers while the plane was on the ground.
August 3, 1961 Continental Air Lines Flight 54 is hijacked to Cuba. The plane is later destroyed in a suicide bombing the next year.
August 9, 1961 Pan Am Flight 501 is hijacked to Cuba.
August 9, 1961 On the same day, a Cubana C-46 experienced an attempted hijacking by 5 Cubans. Two guards on the plane tried to stop him. A gun battle killed the captain, a hijacker and one guard. The plane made an emergency landing in a sugar cane field.
October 26, 1965 National Airlines Flight 209 is hijacked by a Cuban with a pellet gun. Wanting to rescue his family in Havana, he is taken down by the crew with axe.
November 17, 1965 National Airlines Flight 30 experiences an attempted hijack by a 16-year-old boy armed with a gun demanding to be taken to Cuba. He fired six shots through the floor before being overpowered.
March 27, 1966 Angel María Betancourt Cueto, armed with a pistol, tries to hijack a plane from Santiago de Cuba to Havana, with 97 persons, in an attempt to reach the U.S. The pilot, Fernando Álvarez Pérez, opposed the hijacking and landed in Havana. The hijacker then killed Álvarez and armed guard Edor Reyes, seriously wounding the copilot Evans Rosales. The event had a large effect on Cubans. The hijacker later was caught and executed.
August 6, 1967 A Aerocondor C-54 is hijacked to Cuba by five Colombians.
September 9, 1967 An Avianca DC-47 was hijacked by three passengers to Cuba.
November 20, 1967 Louis Gabor Babler, born in Hungary, successfully hijacks a Crescent Airline Piper Apache from Hollywood, Florida to Cuba; the plane was scheduled to go to the Bahamas.
