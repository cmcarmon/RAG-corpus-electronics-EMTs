Parietal-occipital, associated with the awake and relaxed subject, prominent with eyes 
closed.
More evident in frontal-parietal leads, seen best when alpha is blocked.
Associated with normal sleep and present in children less than 1 year old, also seen in 
organic brain disease.
Parietal-temporal, prominent in children 2 to 5 years old.
Waxing and waning of a sinusoidal-like wave having the envelope that resembles a 
spindle, seen during sleep.
Visually evoked, low-amplitude, occipital wave, resulting from recognition of a new 
visual image.
Sharp wave (spike) followed by rounded wave associated with petit mal epilepsy.
