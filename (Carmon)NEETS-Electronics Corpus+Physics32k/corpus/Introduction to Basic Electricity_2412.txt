Direct current (DC), as its name  implies, is a 
current that flows in one direction only.  Batteries 
 develop this type of energy. The second type of  energy 
used today to perform useful work is  alternating 
 current (AC). Unlike its converse, direct current, the 
direction of alternating current flow reverses with 
regularity.
