Devices for Bone and Cartilage Repair
In the United States medical devices are approved for clinical use only after it has been shown to the satisfaction
of the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) that they are not only safe, but also effective. This is a much
more  stringent  requirement  than  the  mere  demonstration  of  safety  demanded  presently  in  most  European
countries (although some EEC countries are considering moving toward approval criteria that are similar to
those used in the United States). The United States laws governing the sale of medical devices for clinical use
are also much more restrictive than the controls over implementation of new surgical procedures that involve
only informal medical peer review. Even organized multi-patient clinical trials require not more than approval
