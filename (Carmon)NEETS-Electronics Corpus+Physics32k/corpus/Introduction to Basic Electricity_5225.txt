Two of the most common soldering defects include 
cold solder connections and disturbed or frac-
tured connections. Cold solder connections are 
the result of withdrawing the soldering iron too soon. 
The solder does not become liquid and leaves a con-
nection that is dull gray in appearance. A fractured or 
disturbed connection is the result of movement of the 
component leads or wire before the solder sets. The 
fractured joint is evident by a granulated and frosty 
appearance with many cracks showing.
