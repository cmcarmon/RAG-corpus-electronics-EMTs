The first sub-orbital space flight was in June 1944, when a V-2 test rocket launched from Peenemünde in Germany reached 189 kilometres altitude.
Bumper 5, a two-stage rocket launched from the White Sands Proving Grounds. On 24 February 1949 the upper stage reached an altitude of 248 miles (399 km) and a speed of 7,553 feet per second (2300 meters per second approx.) which is nearly Mach-7.
USSR — Energia, 1986, Polyus payload failed to reach orbit; this was the most massive object launched into sub-orbital spaceflight to date
