Figure 21-8 shows a triangular	waveform. It 
consists of positive and negative ramps of equal slope. 
During the positive alternation, the waveform rises at a 
linear rate from 0 to its peak value and then decreases 
back to 0. During the negative alternation, the wave-
form declines in the negative direction at a linear rate 
to peak value and then returns to 0. The period of this 
waveform is measured from peak to peak. Triangular 
waveforms are used primarily as electronic signals.
