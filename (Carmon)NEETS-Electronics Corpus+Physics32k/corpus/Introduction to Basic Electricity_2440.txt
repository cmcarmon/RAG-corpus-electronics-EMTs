A mathematical process called the root-mean-square 
(rms) process can be used to determine the effective 
value . Therefore, the effective value is also referred 
to as the rms	value. Using the rms process shows 
that the effective value of a sine wave is equal to 0.707 
times the peak value. When an AC voltage or current 
is given or measured, it is assumed to be the effective 
value unless otherwise indicated. Most meters are cali-
brated to indicate the effective, or rms, value of voltage 
and current.
