This corresponds to the step-like Fermi-Dirac distribution at absolute zero. If there are several degenerate or close to degenerate eigenstates at the Fermi level, it is possible to get convergence problems, since very small perturbations may change the electron occupation. One way of damping these oscillations is to smear the electrons, i.e. allowing fractional occupancies. One approach of doing this is to assign a finite temperature to the electron Fermi-Dirac distribution. Other ways is to assign a cumulative Gaussian distribution of the electrons or using a Methfessel-Paxton method.
