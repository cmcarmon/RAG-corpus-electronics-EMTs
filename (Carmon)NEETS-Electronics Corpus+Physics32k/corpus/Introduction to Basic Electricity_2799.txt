The­capacitive­part­of­the­circuit­consumes­no­
power,­yet­multiplying­the­source­voltage­by­circuit­
current­yields­an­answer­of­112­watts.­The­difference­is­
accounted­for­by­the­difference­in­phases­between­the­
various­voltages.­As­opposed­to­true­power,­the­figure­
obtained­by­multiplying­the­source­voltage­and­cur-
rent­is­known­as­apparent power,­and­it­is­specified­
as­112­VA,­or­112­volt-amperes.­The­true­power­of­the­
circuit­can­never­be­greater­than­the­apparent­power.­
The­ratio­of­true­power­in­watts­to­apparent­power­in­
volt-amps­is­called­the­power factor.
