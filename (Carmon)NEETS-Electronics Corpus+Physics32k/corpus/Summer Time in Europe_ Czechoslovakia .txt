Czech lands and Slovakia used summer time in 1916, 1917 and 1918 under the Austro-Hungarian Empire during World War I.
Protectorate of Bohemia and Moravia used summer time since 1940. In 1940/1941 and 1941/1942, the summer time was kept continuously even during the winter. The Slovak State during World War II also used summer time.

Czechoslovakia used summer time since 1979 up to 1992 (year of its dissolution). Czech Republic and Slovak Republic continue to use the summer time.
